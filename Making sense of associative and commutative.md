### Commutative
The term *commutative* comes from the Latin word *commutare*, which means *to change/exchange*. 
- *com* means together
- *mutare* means to change. 
Commutative $\to$ To change together
The com-mutative property means that, when we change the order of elements in an operation, the final result stays the same. 
In other words, **order does not matter**. 
$$
a + b = b + a
$$
### Associative
The term associative comes from associate, which is a synonym for group or *pair*.
The associative property means that the way we pair elements in an operation does not affect the final results.
$$
(a+b)+c=a+(b+c)
$$

Some properties are both commutative and associative, such as bitwise AND, OR, XOR
Others are associative but not commutative, such as matrix multiplication, [[Why is function composition associative|function composition|]]
or commutative but not associative, such as taking the average (mean), the rock-paper-scissor game, 
or neither, such as subtraction
